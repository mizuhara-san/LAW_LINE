Legal Resources for Children

Key laws protecting child rights, education, and welfare.

Child Labour Prohibition

Children below 14 cannot work in hazardous occupations.

  • Report child labour to police or Labour Inspector.
  • Rescue and rehabilitation by government.
  • Free education compulsory up to age 14.

Reference: Child Labour (Prohibition & Regulation) Act, 1986 (amended 2016)

Protection from Abuse

POCSO Act for sexual offences against children.

  • Report to police or Childline (1098).
  • Special courts for speedy trial.
  • Child-friendly procedures.

Reference: Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012

Right to Education

Free and compulsory education for ages 6-14.

  • No school can deny admission.
  • 25% reservation in private schools for disadvantaged.
  • File complaint with local authority if denied.

Reference: Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009
